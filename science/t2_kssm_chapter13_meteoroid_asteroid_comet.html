13.1 · Other Objects in the Solar System13.1 · 太阳系中的其他天体

Three objects, three identities三种天体，三种身份

Besides stars and planets, meteoroids, asteroids and comets travel through the solar system. Each has its own size, composition, temperature and speed. 除了恒星和行星，流星体、小行星和彗星也在太阳系中运行。它们各自拥有不同的大小、组成、温度和速度。

Movement of a Meteoroid流星体的运行

One rock, three names同一块岩石，三个名字

A meteoroid only changes its name as it changes what it's doing — floating, burning, or landing. 流星体的名字会随着它所处的状态而改变——漂浮、燃烧或落地。

Meteoroid流星体

A rock/metal fragment floating in outer space, travelling around the Sun.在外太空漂浮、绕太阳运行的岩石／金属碎片。

Meteor流星

Enters Earth's atmosphere; friction with air produces heat until it burns — a "streak of light".进入地球大气层；与空气摩擦产生热量并燃烧——形成「一道光」。

Meteorite陨石

A meteor that survives and reaches Earth's surface, sometimes forming a crater.未完全燃烧、抵达地球表面的流星，有时会形成陨石坑。

Meteor shower流星雨
Happens when many meteors enter Earth at the same time.许多流星同时进入地球时发生的现象。
Dinosaur link恐龙灭绝之说
Paleontologists believe a 10 km-wide asteroid impact caused dinosaur extinction.古生物学家认为一个直径10公里的小行星撞击导致了恐龙灭绝。
Movement of an Asteroid小行星的运行

Life in the asteroid belt小行星带中的运行

1

Most asteroids move at ~25 km s⁻¹ in the asteroid belt, between the orbits of Mars & Jupiter.大多数小行星以约25 km/s的速度在火星与木星轨道之间的小行星带中运行。

2

Some orbit outside the belt — near or even intersecting Earth's orbit: the Apollo, Amor and Aten groups.部分小行星轨道在带外——甚至与地球轨道相交：阿波罗群、阿莫尔群与阿登群。

3

Collisions near or crossing Earth's orbit can result in the destruction of living things on Earth.在地球轨道附近或相交处发生的碰撞可能导致地球上生物的毁灭。

Movement of a Comet彗星的运行

The tail always points away from the Sun彗尾始终背向太阳

Comets travel between the Kuiper belt and the Oort cloud at 10–70 km s⁻¹. Watch the tail flip direction as the comet orbits the Sun. 彗星以10–70 km/s的速度在柯伊伯带与奥尔特云之间运行。观察彗尾在绕日运行时如何转向。

1

A comet moves at 10–70 km s⁻¹, ranging between the Kuiper belt and the Oort cloud.彗星以10–70 km/s的速度运行，范围在柯伊伯带与奥尔特云之间。

2

Closer to the Sun, it speeds up — heat melts and vaporises ice, growing a long tail.越靠近太阳，速度越快——热量使冰融化并汽化，形成长长的彗尾。

3

The tail always points away from the Sun, blown by the solar wind — not by the direction of travel.彗尾因太阳风的吹动而始终背向太阳——而非取决于运行方向。

4

Strong gravitational pull from outer planets can easily fling a comet out of its orbit.外行星强大的引力很容易使彗星脱离原来的轨道。

5

A comet's orbit crossing Earth's carries a risk of colliding with Earth at high speed.若彗星轨道与地球轨道相交，就有可能以高速与地球相撞。

Applying What We Know知识应用

Protecting the Earth from asteroid impacts保护地球免受小行星撞击

Scientists constantly monitor asteroids to ensure their orbits stay a safe distance from Earth. Warnings are issued if an asteroid's path risks colliding with Earth — it may then be destroyed or have its course changed. In Malaysia, ANGKASA (the National Space Agency) runs the Near-Earth Object Programme to monitor and develop this science locally. 科学家持续监测小行星，确保它们的轨道与地球保持安全距离。若某小行星的路径有撞击地球的风险，就会发出警报——之后可能将其摧毁或改变其运行路线。在马来西亚，国家太空局（ANGKASA）负责推动与监督「近地天体计划」的太空科学发展。

🛰️ Change its course🛰️ 改变其运行路线

A spacecraft nudges the asteroid so it slips past Earth instead of hitting it.利用太空船轻推小行星，使其偏离原路径而不撞上地球。

💥 Break it apart💥 使其碎裂

Explosives attached to the asteroid, or a bomb fired at it, break it into smaller, safer fragments.在小行星上安装炸药，或向其发射炸弹，使其碎裂成较小、较安全的碎片。

🔭 Early warning🔭 提早预警

Continuous monitoring gives scientists time to plan a response before impact.持续监测让科学家有足够时间在撞击发生前作出应对。

🌍 Impact scale matters🌍 撞击规模决定后果

<10 km asteroid: regional destruction (~100 km radius). ≥10 km asteroid: risk of mass extinction, as with the dinosaurs.小于10公里的小行星：区域性破坏（半径约100公里）。10公里或以上：可能造成如恐龙般的物种大灭绝。
