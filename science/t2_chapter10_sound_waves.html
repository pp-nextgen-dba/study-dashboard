10.1
Sound & Vibration
声音与振动
⌄
🔵 English
Sound is a form of energy produced by vibrations. When an object vibrates, kinetic energy turns into sound energy.

• Musical instruments such as guitars and violins produce sounds through vibrations.
• When we talk, the vocal cords in the throat vibrate to produce our voice.
🟢 中文
声音是由振动产生的一种能量形式。当物体振动时，动能转化为声能。

• 吉他和小提琴等乐器通过振动产生声音。
• 说话时，喉部的声带振动产生我们的声音。
🌬️
10.1
Propagation of Sound — Needs a Medium
声音的传播——需要介质
⌄
🔵 Vacuum Experiment (Bell Jar)
As air is sucked out of the bell jar, the ringing of the alarm bell becomes softer and softer until no sound is heard.
In a vacuum condition, gas particles are not present to act as the medium for propagation.

Conclusion: Sound waves require a medium to propagate. Sound cannot propagate through a vacuum.
🟢 真空实验（钟形罐）
当空气被抽出钟形罐时，警铃声变得越来越轻，直到听不到声音。
在真空状态下，没有气体粒子作为传播介质。

结论：声波需要介质才能传播。声音不能在真空中传播。
🔵 Speed in Different Mediums
Plastic containers filled with air (gas), water (liquid), flour (solid) — the loudest alarm is heard through the flour (solid), then water (liquid), then air (gas).

Conclusion: Sound is transmitted fastest through solids, followed by liquids, then gases.
🟢 不同介质中的速度
装有空气（气体）、水（液体）、面粉（固体）的塑料容器——通过面粉（固体）听到的警报声最大，然后是水（液体），再是空气（气体）。

结论：声音在固体中传播最快，其次是液体，然后是气体。
🟢 Solids / 固体
Particles arranged very closely together → vibration transfers very rapidly.
粒子排列非常紧密→振动传递非常迅速。
🔵 Liquids / 液体
Particles arranged loosely → transfer occurs less rapidly than solids.
粒子排列松散→传递速度比固体慢。
🔵 Gases / Why Slowest
Sound waves travel slowest through gases because particles are arranged very far apart. The transfer of vibration from one particle to another occurs very slowly through gases.
🟢 气体/为何最慢
声波在气体中传播最慢，因为粒子排列得非常分散。气体中粒子间振动的传递非常缓慢。
📝 Memory — Speed of Sound
Solid > Liquid > Gas (fastest → slowest) 固体>液体>气体（最快→最慢）

10.1
Transfer of Sound — 4-Step Process
声音传递——4步过程
⌄
Transfer of Sound Waves / 声波传递
① When an object vibrates, air molecules near the surface also vibrate and collide with nearby air molecules.
当物体振动时，表面附近的空气分子也会振动并与附近的空气分子碰撞。
② This vibration is transferred from one molecule to another molecule beside it in the form of waves.
这种振动以波的形式从一个分子传递到旁边的另一个分子。
③ The vibrating object produces sound that can be heard by the listener when the vibration reaches the ear.
当振动到达耳朵时，振动物体产生的声音就能被听者听到。
🔵 Key Facts
• Sound requires a medium to propagate from one place to another.
• Sound can propagate through solids, liquids and gases.
• Sound propagation requires the vibration of particles in a matter.
• Sound cannot propagate through a vacuum because there are no particles to transmit the vibration.
🟢 关键事实
• 声音需要介质从一处传播到另一处。
• 声音可以通过固体、液体和气体传播。
• 声音传播需要物质中粒子的振动。
• 声音不能在真空中传播，因为没有粒子来传递振动。
🔵 Real-Life Examples
• Sound of a heartbeat transferred through a stethoscope from patient to doctor's ears.
• Sound made by dolphins transferred through seawater.
• Sound of racing cars heard by spectators through air.
🟢 生活实例
• 心跳声通过听诊器从病人传递到医生的耳朵。
• 海豚发出的声音通过海水传播。
• 观众通过空气听到赛车的声音。

10.1
Reflection & Absorption — Surfaces
反射与吸收——表面材质
⌄
🔵 Stopwatch Experiment
Sound of ticking stopwatch heard: loudest when reflected by metal sheet, clearly when reflected by wooden plank, softly when a towel is used.

Conclusion: Wood and metals have hard and smooth surfaces that reflect sound well. Towels have soft and rough surfaces that absorb sound well.
🟢 秒表实验
听到的秒表滴答声：被金属板反射时最响，被木板反射时清晰，使用毛巾时变轻。

结论：木材和金属表面坚硬光滑，能很好地反射声音。毛巾表面柔软粗糙，能很好地吸收声音。
🟣 Good Reflectors / 良好反射体
Hard and smooth surfaces. Examples: Concrete walls, glass, metals.
坚硬光滑的表面。例子：混凝土墙、玻璃、金属。
🟢 Good Absorbers / 良好吸收体
Soft and rough surfaces. Examples: Carpets, curtains, cotton, sponges, rubber, soft boards.
柔软粗糙的表面。例子：地毯、窗帘、棉花、海绵、橡胶、软板。
📝 Memory — Surfaces
Hard + smooth = good reflector, poor absorber 硬+光滑=好反射，差吸收 Soft + rough = good absorber, poor reflector 软+粗糙=好吸收，差反射

10.2
Waveform Properties (C.R.O.)
波形特性（阴极射线示波器）
⌄
🔵 English
The various types of sounds vary in loudness and pitch.
• Loudness depends on the amplitude.
• Pitch depends on the frequency.
The waveform pattern can be observed on a Cathode Ray Oscilloscope (C.R.O.).
🟢 中文
各种声音在音量和音调上各不相同。
• 音量取决于振幅。
• 音调取决于频率。
波形图案可以在阴极射线示波器（C.R.O.）上观察到。
Crest
波峰——波形最高点
Trough
波谷——波形最低点
Wavelength
波长——两个连续波峰（或波谷）间的距离
Amplitude
振幅——从平衡点到波峰/波谷的最大位移值
Frequency
频率——每秒完整波数；1频率=每秒1波

10.2
Loudness & Pitch in Musical Instruments
乐器中的音量与音调
⌄
🔵 Guitar (Strumming)
Stronger strumming → larger amplitude → louder sound.
Weaker strumming → smaller amplitude → weaker sound.
🟢 吉他（弹奏）
弹奏力度更强→振幅更大→声音更响。
弹奏力度更弱→振幅更小→声音更弱。
🔵 Strings (Thickness, Tension, Length)
• Thickness: Thicker string → lower frequency → lower pitch.
• Tension: Greater tension → higher pitch.
• Length: Longer string → lower pitch.
🟢 弦乐（粗细、张力、长度）
• 粗细：弦越粗→频率越低→音调越低。
• 张力：张力越大→音调越高。
• 长度：弦越长→音调越低。
Instrument / 乐器Characteristic / 特征
TubaLow-pitched, produces low frequency sound.低音调，产生低频声音。
TrumpetHigh-pitched, produces high frequency sound.高音调，产生高频声音。
DrumStronger drumbeat → greater vibration → louder sound.击鼓力度越强→振动越大→声音越响。
RecorderPitch changed by covering holes — changes vibrations of air per second (frequency).通过覆盖孔位改变音调——改变每秒空气振动次数（频率）。

Percussions such as gongs and drums are non-pitched instruments.
🟢 科学小知识
锣和鼓等打击乐器属于无固定音调乐器。

10.2
Doppler Effect
多普勒效应
⌄
🔵 Definition
The Doppler effect is the change in the frequency of sound waves caused by the relative movement of the source of sound, the observer, or both.

This occurs when the source of sound moves nearer to or away from the observer; or when the observer moves towards or away from the source.
🟢 定义
多普勒效应是由声源、观察者或两者的相对运动引起的声波频率变化。

当声源靠近或远离观察者时；或当观察者靠近或远离声源时，会发生这种现象。
🔴 Ambulance Moves Away
Frequency of siren decreases. Pitch heard decreases. Longer distance → lower frequency → lower pitch.
警报声频率降低，音调降低。距离越远→频率越低→音调越低。
🟢 Ambulance Moves Nearer
Frequency of siren increases. Pitch heard increases. Shorter distance → higher frequency → higher pitch.
警报声频率增加，音调增加。距离越近→频率越高→音调越高。
🔵 Why Doppler Effect Occurs
Waves are compressed into a lesser space in the direction the source is heading.
• Source & observer approaching → observed frequency is higher than emitted frequency.
• Source & observer moving away → observed frequency is lower than emitted frequency.
🟢 为何发生多普勒效应
波在声源前进方向上被压缩到更小的空间内。
• 声源和观察者互相靠近→观测频率高于发出频率。
• 声源和观察者互相远离→观测频率低于发出频率。

10.3
Echo — Definition & Surfaces
回声——定义与表面材质
⌄
🔵 English
The reflection of sound waves from the surface of an object is known as an echo. The strength of an echo depends on the type of surface on which the sound waves are reflected.

Echoes can be heard in enclosed areas such as caves, tunnels, gorges and halls. An echo cannot be produced when sound is absorbed by the surface.
🟢 中文
物体表面反射的声波被称为回声。回声的强度取决于声波反射的表面类型。

回声可以在洞穴、隧道、峡谷和大厅等封闭区域听到。当声音被表面吸收时，无法产生回声。
Type of Surface / 表面类型 Characteristic / 特征 Effect of Echo / 回声效果
Smooth, flat, hard
光滑、平坦、坚硬		 Good reflectors but weak absorbers. Examples: rock, walls, concrete, glass, metals. 良好反射体但吸收弱。例子：岩石、墙壁、混凝土、玻璃、金属。 Strong echoes / 强回声
Rough, hollow, soft
粗糙、空心、柔软		 Weak reflectors but good absorbers. Examples: carpets, cushions, thick curtains, sponges, cork. 反射弱但吸收良好。例子：地毯、垫子、厚窗帘、海绵、软木。 Weak echoes / 弱回声
🔵 Echo Direction
The reflected echo sounds the same as the original sound but takes some time to reach the listener's ears.
The direction of the original wave is opposite to the direction of the echo.
🟢 回声方向
反射回声与原声相同，但需要一些时间才能到达听者的耳朵。
原始波的方向与回声的方向相反。

10.3
Application — SONAR & Reflection
应用——声纳与反射
⌄
🔵 3 Wave Frequency Ranges
• Infrasonic wave: < 20 Hz. Heard by elephants, rhinoceroses, giraffes.
• Audio sonic wave: 20 Hz – 20 kHz. Heard by humans.
• Ultrasonic wave: > 20 kHz. Heard by bats and dolphins, used for navigation.
🟢 三种频率范围
• 低频声波（次声波）：<20 Hz。由象、犀牛、长颈鹿听到。
• 声波（可闻声）：20 Hz – 20 kHz。人类可听到。
• 超声波：>20 kHz。蝙蝠和海豚可听到，用于导航。
🔵 SONAR (Sound Navigation and Ranging)
A reflection technology used to: detect underwater objects in shipping, detect fish location for fishermen, detect sea depth so boats can sail safely.
🟢 声纳（声音导航与测距）
一种反射技术，用于：检测航运中的水下物体、帮助渔民检测鱼群位置、检测海水深度以便船只安全航行。

10.3
Sonogram & Echolocation
超声波图像与回声定位
⌄
🔵 Sonogram
A sonogram is an image produced by an ultrasound scanning machine. An ultrasound transducer turns electrical energy into sound energy producing ultrasonic waves. These waves are reflected by the foetus and internal organs, and the echoes are received by the transducer. The physical characteristics and gender of the foetus can be identified.
🟢 超声波图像
超声波图像是由超声波扫描机产生的图像。超声波传感器将电能转化为声能，产生超声波。这些波被胎儿和内脏器官反射，回声由传感器接收。可以由此识别胎儿的身体特征和性别。
🔵 Echolocation (Bats & Dolphins)
Bats cannot see clearly in the dark. Bats emit ultrasonic waves and detect reflected waves (echo) from their prey. This technique is known as echolocation. Bats also use echolocation to estimate distances and avoid obstacles when flying.
🟢 回声定位（蝙蝠与海豚）
蝙蝠在黑暗中看不清楚。蝙蝠发出超声波并检测猎物反射的波（回声）。这种技术称为回声定位。蝙蝠也利用回声定位来估计距离并在飞行时避开障碍物。

10.3
Limitations of Hearing & Hearing Range
听觉限制与听觉范围
⌄
🔵 English
The range of hearing is the sound frequency that can be detected by the ears. Human ears can detect frequencies within the range of 20 Hz to 20 000 Hz. Animals such as dogs, bats and dolphins have a higher range of hearing than humans.
🟢 中文
听觉范围是耳朵能检测到的声音频率。人耳可检测20 Hz至20000 Hz范围内的频率。狗、蝙蝠和海豚等动物的听觉范围比人类更广。

Rat 鼠900 Hz – 79 kHz
Bird 鸟1 kHz – 4 kHz
Frog 青蛙50 Hz – 4 kHz
Crocodile 鳄鱼16 Hz – 18 kHz
Dog 狗67 Hz – 45 kHz
Elephant 象16 Hz – 12 kHz
Whale 鲸14 Hz – 36 Hz
Human 人类20 Hz – 20 kHz
🔵 Devices to Overcome Hearing Limitations
• Hearing aid: helps individuals with hearing problems by amplifying sound entering the ear.
• Megaphone: amplifies a person's voice and directs it so it can be heard from afar.
• Stethoscope: enables doctors to listen to a patient's heartbeat.
🟢 克服听觉限制的设备
• 助听器：通过放大进入耳朵的声音帮助听力有问题的人。
• 扬声器：放大一个人的声音并将其传向远处。
• 听诊器：使医生能够听到病人的心跳。
